# 1:1 Meeting Templates and Notes

## Standard 1:1 Template

### Alex — High performer, path to Tech Lead

**Focus areas:** credible TL scope, stakeholder exposure, delegation and “good enough” shipping, facilitation practice.

**Typical discussion topics:** leading a cross-team slice, mentoring Casey with clear boundaries, how decisions get made when PM/design disagree, promotion timing vs. readiness.

**Sample questions:**

- “If you were Tech Lead next month, which two decisions would you own end-to-end?”
- “Where do you want *less* perfectionism and *more* throughput this quarter?”
- “Who do you need a stronger working relationship with outside engineering?”

**Cadence note:** alternate “execution” 1:1s with “leadership craft” 1:1s so career ambition does not crowd out delivery feedback.

---

### Jordan — Developing; complex tasks and confidence

**Focus areas:** scaffolding for hard tasks, shrinking feedback loops, celebrating small wins, psychological safety when stuck.

**Typical discussion topics:** which part of the task is unclear (requirements vs. design vs. implementation), pairing vs. solo time, one skill to deepen this month.

**Sample questions:**

- “On a scale of clarity: what is the *smallest* next step you could do in the next 48 hours?”
- “When you got stuck last time, what would have helped sooner — a spike, a diagram, or pairing?”
- “What kind of tasks feel energizing vs. draining right now?”

**Cadence note:** end many 1:1s with one concrete next step and a named buddy or resource.

---

### Sam — Solid senior, coasting / comfort zone

**Focus areas:** intrinsic motivation, stretch without shame, impact beyond individual output, sharing experience across the team.

**Typical discussion topics:** one deliberate stretch initiative, mentoring or reviewing in areas they do not “need” for themselves, what “interesting” means for them now.

**Sample questions:**

- “What work would make this quarter feel worth it to you personally?”
- “If you had to teach the team one thing from your tenure here, what would it be?”
- “What would you stop doing if you trusted the team to carry it?”

**Cadence note:** avoid lecturing; co-design one stretch with a clear definition of done.

---

### Casey — Promising junior; building independence after pairing

**Focus areas:** graduated responsibility, owning a thin vertical slice, asking for help without blocking, written communication.

**Typical discussion topics:** first solo ticket with guardrails, how they prepare for PR review, time-boxing spikes.

**Sample questions:**

- “What part of the last task did *you* own vs. lean on your pair?”
- “What would ‘independent enough’ look like for the next sprint?”
- “How do you want me to challenge you when I think you can go one step further?”

**Cadence note:** pair success with explicit “solo scope” each sprint.

---

### Morgan — High performer, strong collaborator

**Focus areas:** recognition, next-level challenge, formalizing informal leadership, avoiding burnout from being everyone’s safety net.

**Typical discussion topics:** owning a gnarly cross-team dependency, leading a quality initiative, documenting collaboration practices.

**Sample questions:**

- “Where is the team relying on you in a way that is not visible?”
- “What is the next problem you want to be *known* for solving here?”
- “What support do you need so helping others does not erode your own goals?”

**Cadence note:** treat recognition as recurring, not one-off.

---

### Riley — Underperforming; personal strain affecting work

**Focus areas:** empathy first, facts second, shared plan, boundaries, HR/EAP where appropriate, shorter feedback loops.

**Typical discussion topics:** sustainable capacity, which commitments are realistic, what “good enough” looks like for the next checkpoint, support options.

**Sample questions:**

- “How are you doing, really — and what is one thing making work harder than it needs to be?”
- “What capacity feels truthful for you over the next two weeks?”
- “What would help you most: smaller scope, different pacing, pairing, or time?”

**Cadence note:** weekly while on a performance support plan; document agreements and review prior action items at the top of each meeting.

## 1:1 Notes Examples

### Alex — Week of 2026

**Duration:** 30 min

**Their topics:**

- Frustrated with lack of a clear promotion timeline; wants explicit Tech Lead path and dates or milestones they can influence  
- Wants to lead the database migration project (ownership, sequencing, cutover, and comms—not only implementation tasks)

**My topics:**

- Reinforced: TL bar includes facilitation, stakeholder tradeoffs, and operational rigor (migrations are a good proving ground), not only technical depth  
- Agreed they will draft a short RFC for the migration: options, risks, rollback, and a recommended phased plan (not endless polish)

**Discussion highlights:**

- Named stakeholders for the migration (DBA/platform, SRE, owning product/engineering leads, consumers of affected services)  
- Separated “promotion process” from “readiness”: I’ll clarify how timelines get set here; they’ll drive evidence (RFC, workshop notes, postmortem-style learnings)  
- Agreed mentoring Casey on *one* bounded area (PR feedback style), not open-ended shadowing

**Action items:**

- Me: share Tech Lead competency checklist + example RFCs; confirm who approves migration scope and promotion checkpoints with my manager  
- Alex: migration RFC draft by 27/Mar/2026 (phases, risk/rollback, comms); 45-min review scheduled  
- Me: intro to DBA/platform and PM for dependency / cutover planning workshop

**Follow-up:** revisit promotion timeline expectations and migration RFC feedback in 4 weeks; deeper “readiness narrative” review in 6 weeks; keep one dedicated career block per month so frustration does not only surface in execution 1:1s.

### Riley — Support conversation (same week)

**Duration:** 30 min

**Their topics:**

- Feeling behind; wants to rebuild trust with the team

**My topics:**

- Facts: missed sprint commitments, elevated bug rate, slower review turnaround — framed as observations, not character  
- Goal: stabilize delivery with a realistic plan

**Discussion highlights:**

- Explored capacity honestly; agreed temporary reduction in sprint load  
- Confirmed weekly check-ins and pairing with Morgan on checkout-related changes  
- Shared EAP / flexible arrangements per company policy (details offline)

**Action items:**

- Riley: attend weekly checkpoint; communicate slip >24h early  
- Me: document 8-week checkpoint criteria (quality, attendance, commitments)  
- Morgan: pairing blocks Tue/Thu afternoons

**Follow-up:** HR-sensitive notes stored per company guidance; review metrics at week 4 and week 8.
